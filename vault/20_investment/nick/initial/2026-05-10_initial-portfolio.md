**Kill conditions:**

**Hold horizon:** 18 months

### 2. AVGO — Broadcom Inc.

This is different from NVDA: AVGO's revenue is contracted, predictable, and tied to hyperscaler design wins not open-market GPU allocation.

**Kill conditions:**
| # | Condition | Metric | Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Customer loss | Google or Meta cancels/transfers next-gen ASIC program from AVGO | Sell |
| 2 | Revenue concentration | Top 2 customers drop below 30% combined revenue (was ~50%) — signals diversification working, or | — |

**ROI driver:** Google XPU next-gen (Trillium successor), Meta MTIA v2 ramp, 400G/800G AI networking.
**Hold horizon:** 18 months

### 3. ASML — ASML Holding NV
**Thesis:** ASML is the only company on Earth that can make EUV lithography machines, which are required to manufacture every advanced chip below 7nm. No country, no company can replicate this in <10 years. The High-NA EUV upgrade cycle (TWINSCAN EXE:5000) is beginning — ASML will charge ~$400M per machine vs ~$200M for current EUV.

**Kill conditions:**
| # | Condition | Metric | Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Geopolitical shock | Netherlands government forced to ban all ASML exports OR US extends export control to cover ASML | Sell immediately |
| 2 | Cycle downturn | Semiconductor capex cycle turns — ASML guidance falls >20% YoY for 2 consecutive quarters | Trim 50% |
| 3 | Technology disruption | New lithography technology (e.g., DSA, e-beam) achieves volume production at sub-3nm | Sell |

**ROI driver:** High-NA EUV ramp starting 2026-2027, Intel 18A/TSMC N2 orders, Korea fab expansion.
**Hold horizon:** 24 months
