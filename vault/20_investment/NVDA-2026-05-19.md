## Moat / ความได้เปรียบในการแข่งขัน

- **CUDA software lock-in:** 15+ year ecosystem — training pipelines, libraries, developer familiarity; switching cost extreme
- **Full-stack advantage:** GPU + NVLink + networking (Mellanox) + software (CUDA/cuDNN/TensorRT) — AMD can match chip but not full stack
- **NVL72/GB200 cluster dominance:** hyperscalers need full cluster, not just chips — NVDA sells complete rack infrastructure
- **T2 kill condition:** viable NVDA alternatives capture >15% hyperscaler training spend

## Bull case

1. **Blackwell demand exceeds supply:** DC revenue est. grew 35% in a single quarter ($53.8B → $72.8B); GB200/GB300 backlog extends into FY2028
2. **China H200 optionality:** NVDA guided Q1 with ZERO China revenue; Jensen announced GTC 2026 purchase orders → any resumption = pure upside to guidance
3. **Q2 guide likely $86B+:** Accelerated hyperscaler capex commitments (AWS/Azure/Google each guiding multi-hundred-billion capex cycles) — NVDA is the primary beneficiary

---

## Bear case (steelman)

1. **Gross margin compression as Blackwell scales:** New architecture transitions historically compress margins 2-4% as yields improve; if GM dips below 73% = pricing or yield problem
2. **China H200 in legal limbo:** Deal can be reversed by Beijing or US Commerce at any time; concentration risk if China demand is priced in by market before deliveries happen
3. **Q2 guidance deceleration risk:** $86B consensus is pricing in acceleration; any guide of $82-84B (miss vs consensus but still growth) triggers de-rating even on clean Q1 beat — happened in prior cycles
