## Bull case

1. **Blackwell demand far exceeds any prior forecast:** DC revenue estimate grew from $53.8B to $72.8B in a single quarter (+35%) — analysts have been consistently under-forecasting. If this pattern holds, actual Q1 revenue likely lands above $80B.

2. **China H200 = undisclosed upside option:** NVDA guided Q1 with zero China DC revenue. Jensen confirmed GTC 2026 purchase orders exist. If any H200 deliveries occurred in Q1 or are confirmed for Q2, this is revenue the street has modeled as $0 — pure beat expansion.

3. **Hyperscaler capex still accelerating:** AWS FY2026 capex guidance: >$100B. Azure: multi-hundred-billion AI buildout. Google: Gemini infrastructure. Meta: $60-65B FY2026. All four have explicitly committed to sustained capex growth through at least FY2028. NVDA is the primary beneficiary — not AMD, not Google TPUs, not custom silicon at scale yet.

---

## Bear case (steelman)

1. **Gross margin decompression is structural, not transient:** Blackwell ramp requires NVDA to absorb yield losses on CoWoS packaging and HBM3e supply constraints. If Q1 GM comes in at 73-74% and Q2 guidance implies further compression, the Blackwell premium evaporates — stock re-rates from 35x forward to 28x, implying ~$175 fair value at current consensus earnings.

2. **China H200 is a time bomb both ways:** If deliveries restart, NVDA becomes re-exposed to US Commerce reversal risk with inventory in transit. If deliveries don't restart, the GTC announcement was noise — and the market may have priced in partial China recovery. This uncertainty keeps a premium in the stock that should not be.

3. **$86B Q2 consensus is a trap:** The market is pricing in continued 30%+ quarterly growth. Any guide of $82-85B (which would still be extraordinary growth) reads as disappointment. NVDA has dropped on "beat and guide in-line with consensus" before. At $225 (30x+ NTM revenue), the embedded expectations leave almost no room for anything less than exceptional.
