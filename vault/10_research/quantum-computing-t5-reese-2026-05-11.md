# Quantum Computing — T5 Research Doc

## Narrative

Quantum computing in 2026 is no longer purely research — but it is also not yet broadly commercial. The sector splits into two distinct timelines: quantum annealing (D-Wave/QBTS) is commercially useful today for constrained optimization; gate-model quantum computing (IonQ, Rigetti, IBM) is on a path to fault tolerance by 2029, with narrowly defined "quantum advantage" demonstrations possible by end-2026.

IonQ is the only pure-play with genuine revenue scale: $130M FY2025 (+202% YoY), $64.7M Q1 2026 (+755% YoY), $470M RPO. It is the anchor of T5 — the only name where commercial momentum is unambiguous. D-Wave (QBTS) is the second strongest: $24.6M FY2025 (+179% YoY), $32.8M YTD 2026 bookings already approaching full-year 2025 totals, including a Fortune 100 QCaaS deal.

Rigetti (RGTI) is the laggard: FY2025 revenue declined 34% YoY to $7.1M. QUBT has no verifiable revenue. IBM has the most credible technical roadmap (qLDPC error correction 10x speedup, one year ahead of schedule) but quantum is a small fraction of a $60B+ enterprise — no pure-play exposure.

The primary risk across T5 is valuation, not technology. Rigetti trades at ~1,025x sales ❓, D-Wave at ~325x ❓, even IonQ prices in near-perfection. The bubble risk is real: if timeline expectations shift by even 1-2 years, multiples compress severely.

## Bull case

1. **IonQ is the first quantum company to cross $100M revenue with 202% YoY growth and $470M RPO.** The RPO metric is the key — it means multi-year contracts, not one-time bookings. Q1 2026 beat guidance by 30%. FY2026 guidance raised to $260-270M (+100%+). System sales (Cambridge 256-qubit, KISTI 100-qubit), government contracts ($39M HALO Space Dev Agency), and QuantumBasel $60M+ expansion validate multiple demand vectors.

2. **D-Wave (QBTS) annealing is commercially viable today — no fault tolerance required.** Optimization problems (logistics, scheduling, drug discovery) can be solved on D-Wave hardware now. $32.8M YTD 2026 bookings (including Fortune 100 QCaaS deal) demonstrate enterprise willingness to pay. 179% FY2025 revenue growth and 471% sequential Q4 bookings growth are real commercial acceleration.

3. **IBM's error correction progress sets the technical credibility floor for the entire sector.** qLDPC decoding at 480 nanoseconds — 10x speedup, one year ahead of schedule. Kookaburra (2026): first module with qLDPC memory + Logical Processing Unit. Starling (2029): 200 logical qubits, 100M error-corrected operations. If IBM demonstrates quantum advantage in even one real workload by end-2026, sector-wide re-rating is possible.

## Bear case

1. **Valuation leaves zero margin for execution shortfall.** Rigetti ~1,025x sales ❓, D-Wave ~325x ❓, IonQ implied perfection at $130M revenue. Motley Fool Dec 2025: "the quantum computing bubble will burst." Five cited reasons: revenue too small, timelines always pushed out, classical computing keeps improving, hype cycles are predictable, and institutional money will eventually rotate out.

2. **Gate-model fault tolerance is 2029 — 3 full years of execution risk before the core thesis proves out.** Three years of quarterly earnings, cash burn, dilutive raises, and timeline revisions before IonQ/Rigetti can claim fault-tolerant advantage. Classical computing (GPU acceleration, better optimization algorithms) keeps improving during this window, potentially closing some quantum use-case gaps.

3. **QUBT and RGTI have material company-specific risks.** RGTI: revenue declined 34% in 2025 while peers grew 100-200% — structural underperformance, not noise. QUBT: no verifiable revenue, short-seller allegations ❓ (unconfirmed details), Wedbush Neutral at $12. These are lottery-ticket tier, not thesis-anchor tier.
