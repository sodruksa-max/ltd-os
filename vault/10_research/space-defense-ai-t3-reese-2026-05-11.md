# Space & Defense AI — T3 Research Doc

## Narrative

T3 covers two distinct sub-themes that share a risk profile (binary, government-dependent, early commercial) but have different moat structures. The first is the space economy ramp — launch infrastructure (RKLB), direct-to-device connectivity (ASTS), and lunar services (LUNR). The second is AI-native defense software and unmanned systems (KTOS, BBAI).

The space sub-theme is being de-risked faster than the market expected. RKLB now has $2.2B+ backlog and 87 Electron launches completed — it is no longer speculative on the launch side. ASTS received FCC commercial authority in April 2026, removing the biggest regulatory overhang. The remaining risks are operational: Neutron first flight (Q4 2026), ASTS satellite count reaching commercial density, and LUNR executing a near-impossible $210M → $900M revenue ramp in 12 months.

The defense sub-theme is more bifurcated. KTOS is the most fundamentally grounded name in T3: $1.35B 2025 revenue, organic growth, profitable, Valkyrie drone program scaling from 8 to 40 units/year. BBAI is the opposite — small contracts, crowded competitive space, insufficient data to assign conviction.

## Bull case

1. **RKLB has a two-layer moat that is now validated by backlog.** Electron (small-lift, 87 launches) is a proven workhorse generating recurring revenue. Neutron (medium-lift, 13,000 kg LEO) targets the megaconstellation market with the May 2026 multi-launch booking from a confidential customer. Backlog >$2.2B. Q1 2026 sold more launches than all of 2025 combined. The DoD relationship is deepening — $190M ❓ block-buy for hypersonic test flights (HASTE program). If Neutron flies Q4 2026 on schedule, RKLB becomes the only vertically integrated launch + spacecraft provider at scale outside SpaceX.

2. **ASTS FCC approval removes the regulatory kill condition and opens the $1T+ mobile connectivity TAM.** FCC granted commercial authority April 22, 2026. BlueBird 6 (largest commercial comms array in LEO) demonstrated 120 Mbps to standard unmodified phones. Carrier partnerships with AT&T and Verizon create a distribution moat SpaceX's consumer-direct model cannot easily replicate — ASTS revenue flows through existing carrier billing relationships. Target 45-60 satellites by end-2026 at 1-2 launches/month cadence.

3. **KTOS is a defense compounder, not a speculative bet.** 2025 revenue $1.35B (+16.6% organic), 2026 guided $1.6-1.7B. Valkyrie production ramping 8→40 units/year by 2027. $447M Space Force contract adds non-drone revenue pillar. This is real revenue growing at defense-premium rates with DoD contractual visibility.

## Bear case

1. **LUNR's $900M 2026 guidance is a near-vertical revenue ramp with extreme execution risk.** Going from $210M to $900M requires flawless delivery on NASA CLPS tasks, Lanteris integration, and new contract ramps simultaneously. NASA Artemis program budget pressure is a macro risk — any Congressional cut to lunar exploration cascades directly to LUNR's revenue base. Adjusted EBITDA (not GAAP) is the metric they're guiding to; GAAP profitability is unconfirmed ❓.

2. **ASTS valuation prices in near-flawless execution at an elevated multiple.** EV/Sales was cited at 228x ❓ (at time of prior analysis — likely compressed since). At any elevated multiple, a satellite launch delay or carrier partnership dispute would trigger 30-50% drawdown. Starlink direct-to-cell is scaling simultaneously with effectively unlimited SpaceX capital — ASTS's carrier-partnership moat is real but fragile if Starlink wins carrier deals too.

3. **BBAI competes in a crowded DoD AI analytics space against Palantir, Booz Allen, and Leidos.** Contracts surfaced are small in scale ($13.2M sole-source, IDIQ vehicles). No revenue scale, cash burn, or balance sheet data confirmed ❓. To be a T3 core hold, BBAI needs a large sole-source win demonstrating defensible niche — current evidence is insufficient.
