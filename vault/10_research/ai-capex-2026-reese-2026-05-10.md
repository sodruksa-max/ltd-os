# AI Capex Cycle 2026 — Who Actually Wins

## Narrative

The AI capex supercycle is accelerating, not pausing. The four largest hyperscalers — MSFT, GOOGL, AMZN, META — collectively guided to ~$700B in 2026 capital expenditure, nearly 3x their 2024 levels. Every company raised or maintained guidance in Q1 2026 earnings. The T1 kill condition (2 consecutive quarters of capex guidance below prior year) has not been triggered — the opposite has occurred.

But within the spend, a structural shift is underway. Custom ASICs are winning a growing share of inference workloads, and NVDA remains dominant in training. The key insight the market misses: the custom ASIC winner is not the hyperscalers themselves — it's AVGO, who designs and manufactures the chips for GOOGL (TPU), META (MTIA), and ByteDance. AVGO is printing $6.5B/quarter in AI semiconductor revenue with a $73B backlog, growing faster than NVDA's data center segment on a percentage basis.

ASML sits one level up: EUV supply constraints (not demand) are the binding factor through 2027. The TSMC High-NA delay to 2029 is a timing risk on the premium upgrade cycle but doesn't threaten the core EUV story.

## Bull case

1. **Capex trajectory leaves no room for thesis doubt right now.** Combined 2026 guidance of ~$700B❓ (estimate, not per-company official total) is a 3x from 2024 levels. Q1 2026 actuals: GOOGL $35.7B, MSFT $31.9B, AMZN $43.2B. META raised full-year guide. Zero evidence of pause at the executive level.

2. **AVGO is the structural beneficiary of custom ASIC shift.** AI semiconductor revenue: $6.5B/quarter Q4 FY2025 (+74% YoY), guided $8.2B for Q1 FY2026. Confirmed XPU customers: GOOGL, META, ByteDance. $73B total backlog. $60B TAM estimate for custom AI silicon by 2026, AVGO projected at ~60% market share. AVGO wins whether hyperscalers buy more GPUs or more custom chips.

3. **ASML: supply-constrained means pricing power intact.** €38.8B backlog + €46.5B performance obligations = multi-year revenue visibility. TSMC 2nm ramp, Samsung P5, Intel 18A all require additional EUV through 2027. China DUV decline (36% → 19% of system sales) is isolated to lower-margin product — EUV is unaffected. TSMC High-NA delay to 2029 ❓ (sourced, but TSMC official statement not directly confirmed) is a timing risk, not a demand collapse.

## Bear case

1. **Overbuild risk is real and was flagged by a hyperscaler CEO.** Satya Nadella (Feb 2026) publicly flagged industry "overbuild" risk. Capex is being depreciated over 6 years vs 3-year traditional server standard — if write-downs are forced, impairment cascade could hit hyperscaler earnings. ROI on AI workloads is not yet proven at scale; cloud revenue growth needs to sustain >40% YoY to justify $700B/year spend.

2. **NVDA's inference share is being eroded directionally, not episodically.** AMZN's Trainium cited at "$50B annualized revenue run rate potential" ❓ (forward estimate, not yet realized). AVGO's ASIC TAM of $60B by 2026 carves out the inference layer — where hyperscalers have the most cost incentive to use custom silicon. Analyst projection: custom ASICs reach 25% of accelerator market by 2035 ❓ (analyst projection, not confirmed data). Slow but directional.

3. **ASML's highest-margin upgrade cycle is delayed 2-3 years.** TSMC postponing High-NA EUV to 2029 means the premium ASP uplift is deferred. ASML fills the gap with EUV volume shipments, which are lower-margin. 2026-2028 revenue grows but mix is less favorable than bulls expected 12 months ago.
