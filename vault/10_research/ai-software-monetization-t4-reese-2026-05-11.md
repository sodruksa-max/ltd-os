# AI Software Monetization — T4 Research Doc

## Narrative

The AI software monetization thesis rests on a structural shift: the SaaS model is migrating from seat-based pricing to consumption/task-based pricing driven by AI agents. All three T4 names are at different stages of this transition — and the transition itself is the risk as much as the opportunity.

PLTR is the clearest AI software compounder right now. Q1 2026 revenue grew 85% YoY (fastest since 2020), U.S. commercial +133% YoY. Its ontology-and-workflow architecture means AIP is the governance layer for AI agents — not just a model API wrapper. The government moat (20 years of clearances, FedRAMP, forward-deployed engineers) is nearly impossible to replicate. The only risk is valuation: at any reasonable peer normalization, the downside is severe.

CRM is executing the transition from seat-to-consumption through Agentforce. 29,000+ deals closed, $800M Agentforce ARR growing at 169%+ YoY. The AWU (Agentic Work Unit) pricing model is the key innovation — it decouples revenue from headcount, which matters as AI reduces enterprise workforce. But the transition period is dangerous: the legacy seat business grows slowly while Agentforce ARR is still a small share of $36B+ total revenue.

SNOW is the data infrastructure play within T4. The thesis is data gravity: enterprise data is already in Snowflake, and running AI on that data inside Snowflake eliminates egress costs and latency. The $200M OpenAI deal brings frontier models natively into Cortex. But Databricks (private, now crossing warehousing territory) and Apache Iceberg (open format enabling external queries on Snowflake data) are the structural threats that could erode the data gravity moat.

## Bull case

1. **PLTR's AIP is the governance layer for enterprise agentic AI — highest-conviction T4 hold.** Q1 2026: 85% total revenue growth, U.S. commercial +133% YoY. FY2026 guidance $7.65B (+71%). AIP boot camps convert to full contracts at high rates. The government segment has a 20-year regulatory moat. As AI agents proliferate in enterprises, the orchestration/audit layer (PLTR's ontology) becomes more valuable, not less.

2. **CRM Agentforce AWU model decouples revenue from headcount — the right architecture for the AI era.** $800M Agentforce ARR (+169% YoY), 29,000+ deals. Consumption-based pricing means CRM can grow even as AI reduces client headcount. Data Cloud unifies customer data across clouds — agents running on unified data are structurally more capable. Embedded in existing workflows (Sales Cloud, Service Cloud, Slack) = low switching friction.

3. **SNOW's data gravity + OpenAI partnership locks AI workloads inside the platform.** 9,100+ Cortex AI accounts. AI workloads growing 200%+ and linked to ~50% of new bookings. $200M OpenAI deal makes frontier models natively available in Cortex — customers don't need to choose between best AI and existing data infrastructure. Every AI inference run on Snowflake consumes credits = revenue without new seat sales.

## Bear case

1. **PLTR valuation is the highest risk — 80%+ downside at peer-group multiple normalization.** Trading at ~80-115x trailing revenue ❓ (varies by source/date). Motley Fool analysis: at software sector median P/S (~10-15x), the stock could fall 80%. If growth decelerates from 85% to even 40% YoY, multiple compression compounds the pain. Jefferies bear target: $70. The government segment carries DOGE/budget-cut macro risk not yet showing in results.

2. **CRM is in a dangerous transition — legacy seat revenue growing slowly while Agentforce is unproven at scale.** Microsoft Copilot (embedded in Dynamics 365 + Teams) is a direct competitor for the same enterprise workflow budget. Talent leakage is a warning signal: Salesforce's former Slack CEO joined OpenAI as CRO. AWU pricing elasticity is unconfirmed ❓ — it is unclear whether $800M Agentforce ARR is fully contracted or includes heavily discounted pilots.

3. **SNOW faces Databricks below and hyperscalers above — data gravity moat is not permanent.** Databricks crossed warehousing revenue that directly attacks SNOW's core. Apache Iceberg open table format allows data to live outside Snowflake and still be queried — if this becomes Fortune 500 default, data gravity weakens materially. SNOW growth decelerating (80%+ → 30% YoY); if trend continues to 20%, valuation pressure follows. $200M OpenAI cost creates gross margin uncertainty ❓.
