# Synthesis: ควรเริ่ม project trading-foundations แบบไหนดี

## Where Proposers AGREE (non-negotiables)

- **Journal before everything else.** All three make `vault/20_investment/_journal/` Module 1 and never turn it off.
- **Pre-trade documentation of stop + target is the highest-value single habit.** Pragmatist says it most clearly; Skeptic's Phase 1 requires it; Optimist's journal template requires it. The expertise lens confirms: pre-trade stops are the structural difference between a journal that builds skill and one that records fiction.
- **No bot code before Month 4 at earliest.** All three agree: automating a strategy you haven't manually proven is building a machine to lose money faster.
- **20+ documented trades is the minimum gate before activating any secondary module.** This number appears independently in both Optimist and Pragmatist.
- **Paper trading psychology ≠ real-money psychology.** Paper win rates overstate real-money performance by 15–30 percentage points in the first real-money quarter.

---

## Where Proposers DIVERGE (actual decisions — not reconcilable, user must choose)

**1. When does content start?**
- Optimist: Month 2, from real trade data, no audience risk at zero followers
- Pragmatist: Month 3-4, only after 3+ explainable trade examples exist
- Skeptic: Month 7+, after first full month of real-money trading only

Note: Skeptic's identity-trap argument applies to Month 3 content nearly as much as Month 1. Optimist's Day-1 content claim is unsupported by cited examples (Humbled Trader and Rayner Teo both started after having a track record).

**2. Is 15h/day an advantage or a risk?**
- Optimist: advantage — compress 2-year curve into 6 months
- Skeptic: risk — free time fills with activity that feels like progress but isn't
Both are true for different user types.

**3. Simple tracker = bot, or separate category?**
- Pragmatist: CSV tracker that calculates win rate ≠ "building a bot." Month 3 activation is not premature.
- Skeptic: any code before Month 4 dilutes focus
The expertise lens sides with Pragmatist: manual calculation with fee adjustments is error-prone enough that a simple calculator is a capital protection tool.

## Hybrid Options

**Hybrid A: Pragmatist spine + Skeptic content gate**
Run Pragmatist's 3-module structure, but override Module 3 content activation to Month 7+ (post first real-money month). Keeps capital protection of pre-trade documentation, the useful CSV tracker, and eliminates identity risk from publishing during paper phase.

**Hybrid B: Skeptic Phase 1 + Pragmatist Month 3 forward**
Run Skeptic's Phase 1 (90 days, journal only, hard stop at <50 complete trades) as a proof-of-commitment filter. If you pass, switch to Pragmatist's module structure from Month 4. Removes the weakness of Skeptic's model having no positive reinforcement for a user who executes well.

**Hybrid C: Optimist flywheel + Skeptic content timing + expertise fee fix**
Use Optimist's integrated approach (journal → screener → content from same asset) but: (a) move content to Month 7+, (b) add fee recalibration (track USD and THB separately, use 1.7–1.8 gross R target), (c) add bidirectional gate (real-money win rate <30% over 15 trades → mandatory 60-day return to paper).

---

## Expertise Lens Findings (Financial Risk)

Four items none of the proposals addressed adequately:

1. **Fee-adjusted R-multiple**: at 2,500 THB position sizes, round-trip fees consume 10–30% of the 500 THB risk unit. The 1.5 gate is gross. Net of fees requires gross R of 1.7–1.8. Must be in journal template from Day 1.

2. **USD/THB currency risk missing**: risk per trade is denominated in USD at execution. Journal must record USD amounts alongside THB equivalents.

3. **Bidirectional real-money gate required**: if real-money win rate falls below 30% over any 15-trade window, mandatory return to paper-only for 60 days. Commit to this rule before Month 7, not at the moment it triggers.

4. **Additional gate metric**: existing win rate + R-multiple must also require ≥3 documented instances of a stop-loss being hit and honored at the stop price (not modified, not held through).

## Hidden Assumptions Surfaced

- Optimist assumed user has self-regulatory capacity to log every trade without external accountability — no structural safeguard, only behavioral commitment
- Pragmatist assumed "setup adherence rate" is measurable from Day 1, but user hasn't defined their 2-3 setups yet — undefined metric for first 4-6 weeks
- Skeptic assumed 90 days of pure journaling is motivationally sustainable — no positive reinforcement mechanism for a user who executes well
- All three assumed the 100K capital stays ring-fenced under emotional pressure (after -42% drawdown on 10K live tranche, the behavioral pressure to "make it back" tests this separation)

## Open Questions for User

1. **What is your primary fear: losing capital, or wasting 6 months of time?** Capital-fear → Hybrid A or Skeptic. Time-waste-fear → Hybrid C.

2. **Have you completed any 30+ day streak of daily writing or journaling before?** If no: Skeptic's 90-day pure-journal phase has a real dropout risk. If yes: you have evidence you can sustain the routine.

3. **Do you currently know what 2-3 momentum setups you are looking for?** If no: Pragmatist's "Day 1 paper trading" requires defining these first.

4. **When your first real-money position hits -20% overnight — what do you do?** (At 2,500 THB position: -500 THB in one session.) If you can't answer without hesitation, gate metrics are not the binding constraint.

5. **How much of the content goal is income/audience vs. learning-by-teaching?** Income → Skeptic's delay has real opportunity cost. Learning-by-teaching → Pragmatist Month 3-4 serves this without identity risk.

## Recommendation Framework (NOT a recommendation)

If you weigh **capital protection** highest → **Hybrid A** (Pragmatist + Skeptic content timing + fee fix)

If you weigh **motivation sustainability** highest → **Hybrid C** (Optimist flywheel + Month 7 content + fee fix + bidirectional gate)

If you weigh **reversibility / minimum regret** highest → **Hybrid B** (Skeptic 90-day filter, then Pragmatist)

If you weigh **simplicity** highest → **Pragmatist as-is** + two expertise additions: (a) gross R target 1.7–1.8, (b) USD/THB tracking in journal

> The binding question is not which proposal is best — it is whether you have documented proof of your own consistency over 30+ days of any daily habit. That answer changes the risk ranking of every option above.
