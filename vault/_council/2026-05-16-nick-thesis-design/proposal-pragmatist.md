# Proposal: Three Tight Theses, Measurable Kills, QQQ Benchmark

## Core thesis

Nick's edge is not stock-picking — it is thesis discipline: structured kill conditions that force selling when the story breaks, before price confirms it. Protect this edge by collapsing 6 theses into 3 that are genuinely non-overlapping, fixing the benchmark to QQQ (the correct peer), and deferring T5 Quantum until a measurable commercial milestone exists.

## Answers to open questions A–F

**A. 3 tight or 6 broad?**
3 tight. Cremers et al. (SSRN 4346421) find that funds outperform only when their portfolio closely matches their stated factor. 6 theses with 30+ tickers collapses into a theme fund, not a thesis fund. T1 and T2 share the same kill condition (hyperscaler capex guidance) and the same primary holding (NVDA). They are one thesis wearing two labels. Merge them.

**B. T5 Quantum — any allocation?**
Zero now. IONQ, RGTI, QBTS have no revenue-based kill condition that can be checked today. A thesis without a falsifiable kill condition is a story. The entry trigger should be explicit and external: "A commercially deployed error-corrected qubit system generates verified pilot revenue." Until that milestone is public, watching costs nothing; owning teaches the wrong lesson.

**C. ISRG as non-AI diversification?**
Not at 65x P/E. ISRG is a genuine moat business — da Vinci installed base, 75% gross margin, 20-year switching-cost advantage. It is legitimately non-correlated to AI capex. But 65x P/E requires surgical volume growth above 15% for 5+ years to justify. Nick's kill condition framework has no valuation anchor anywhere in the current system. Add ISRG to Watch with a price trigger: P/E < 50x, or a specific catalyst (new indication FDA approval, China volume recovery). Not a current buy.

**D. Nick's real edge?**
Kill condition discipline — not idea generation. The market already prices NVDA's dominance. Nick's edge is the structured exit: selling when the kill condition fires before price confirms it. This is process edge, not information edge. Two fixes required to protect it: (1) every kill condition must name a data source and a check date, not just a qualitative threshold; (2) PLTR kill condition 3 ("stock -40% → HOLD") must be removed — a price-based hold rule that overrides thesis checks defeats the entire system.

**E. SPY or QQQ benchmark?**
QQQ — specifically the 50% QQQM + 50% SOXX blend already written into nick-soul.md. Using SPY for a semicon-heavy portfolio means Nick can underperform QQQ by 8% while claiming alpha vs SPY. That is tech beta, not alpha. The correct benchmark is the peer group Nick is actually competing against. The soul.md already has the right answer — it is not being applied consistently.

**F. Post-AI-capex world (2028+)?**
Build thesis sunset phases now, not later. TA (AI Monopoly Infrastructure) should have a documented phase gate at 24 months (mid-2028): if hyperscaler capex growth decelerates to single-digit YoY, the supercycle thesis has matured. At that point TA rotates from "growth via spend increase" to "moat via installed base monetization." ASML survives this transition (consumables + service revenue are durable); NVDA requires re-evaluation (does CUDA lock-in generate datacenter refresh revenue independent of new AI build-out?). Writing this phase gate now means Nick exits on thesis evolution, not panic.

- Mag-7 vs S&P 493 (2023-2024): +159% vs +28%. Concentration in the right structural theme works — but only when held with conviction through drawdowns. Conviction requires falsifiability.
- Goldman Sachs 2025: AI sentiment shifts now create correlated drawdowns across the full AI infrastructure stack. NVDA 30% + AVGO 25% = 55% in one macro factor. Reducing combined TA to 50% with AVGO as optional add (not core) is the median-safe path.
- Oxford Review of Finance (2025): retail investors capture momentum premium better than institutions. Nick's 6-month+ hold horizon is the correct implementation of this finding. Do not churn.

## What I am not doing and why

**Not recommending 6 theses.** More theses dilutes conviction and removes the only edge Nick has. A 10-stock portfolio across 6 theses is a closet QQQ with higher turnover costs and lower discipline.

**Not recommending any T5 Quantum position.** Pre-commercial assets with no measurable kill condition are not theses — they are lottery tickets. They teach the wrong lesson at exactly the wrong time in Nick's development as an investor.

**Not recommending ISRG now.** It is a great business at a price that requires a specific outcome. Without a valuation anchor in the kill framework, ISRG becomes "hold forever because moat." That is a story, not a thesis.

**Not recommending SPY as benchmark.** Honest measurement requires the right peer. SPY lets a tech-concentrated portfolio hide sector tailwinds as alpha. QQQ/SOXX blend is the intellectually honest choice — and it is already in nick-soul.md.
