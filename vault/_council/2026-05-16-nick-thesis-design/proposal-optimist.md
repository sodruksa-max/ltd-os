# Proposal: Four-Thesis Conviction Engine with Built-In Sunset Dates

## Core thesis

Nick's current instincts are already correct — the portfolio is concentrated, thesis-driven, and disciplined. The gap is framework clarity, not a structural overhaul. Codify what's working, add sunset dates for post-AI-capex, and deploy the idle cash into a small venture tier. This turns a good start into a replicable learning system.

Consolidate to 4 active theses, each with a hard sunset date and explicit source for kill-condition data. Redeploy current 10% cash: 7% into ISRG, 3% into IONQ.

## Answer to each open question (A–F)

**A. 3 tight theses or 6 broad ones?**
Four, not six. T1 and T2 share kill conditions, overlap on NVDA, and bet on the same macro driver — hyperscaler capex. Merge them into one "Semiconductor Moats" thesis with a single kill condition source. T6 Robotics has zero capital; either fund it or retire it. Funding it through ISRG activates a real position without adding a sixth thesis with no capital behind it. Six theses with only four allocations is administrative overhead — it dilutes Nick's analytical attention without diversifying actual risk.

**B. T5 Quantum — worth any allocation?**
Yes, but ring-fenced as an explicit venture bet at 3% of NAV with a hard kill date of 2027-Q1. If no commercially viable error-corrected qubit system is demonstrated by then, the position closes — no extensions, no rationalizations. The asymmetric upside is real: if Google or IBM announces a fault-tolerant breakthrough, IONQ-class names can move 3-5x in weeks. The bet costs $300 in paper capital. The learning value of holding a pre-commercial thesis through a binary catalyst event is worth more than the dollar amount at risk. One name only: IONQ has the deepest commercial pipeline.

**C. Should ISRG be added?**
Yes, at 7%. ISRG is not an expensive growth stock in different clothes. It is a 20-year installed base moat with 65%+ gross margins and a consumables revenue stream that compounds independently of AI sentiment. In any scenario where AI infrastructure stocks correct 30-40% on a macro shock, ISRG holds — or rises, as defensive healthcare rotations are common in risk-off environments. This gives Nick a live case study in genuine non-correlation. Kill condition: da Vinci loses FDA approval for a major procedure category, or robot-assisted surgery market share reverses for 2 consecutive years.

**D. What is Nick's real edge?**
Structured kill conditions tied to falsifiable metrics. The market prices narrative and sentiment. Nick's edge is exiting on evidence rather than emotion. Most retail investors hold until they panic or hold forever with no exit logic. Kill conditions pre-commit Nick to the criteria for being wrong — before entering, not after losing money. Over a 2-3 year learning horizon, this instills the single most valuable investment habit. It is a genuine edge: even many professional fund managers do not have written, measurable exit criteria at the position level.

**E. SPY or QQQ benchmark?**
QQQ — and more specifically, the 50% QQQM + 50% SOXX blend already in nick-soul.md. Beating SPY with a 60% semiconductor portfolio in a tech bull market proves nothing. Beating QQQ proves Nick's thesis selection adds alpha beyond passive tech exposure. If Nick cannot outperform QQQ over 2 years, the honest conclusion is: own QQQM passively. That is a valuable lesson too, and it is the lesson the benchmark is designed to surface.

**F. Post-AI-capex world (2028+)?**
Sunset every thesis now. The AI capex supercycle thesis expires 2028-Q1 unless renewed by evidence. Nick should open watch positions in the KB on 2028+ candidates by mid-2027: energy infrastructure for AI datacenters, sovereign AI (government-owned compute outside US hyperscalers), edge AI (on-device inference). Budget one /stock-content run per quarter for a 2028+ candidate. The habit of constructing the next thesis before the current one expires is the skill that distinguishes multi-year investors from one-cycle holders.

## Why this approach wins

The concentrated thesis investor who holds through noise and exits on evidence is one of the most consistently well-performing profiles in public markets. The failure mode is not concentration — it is failing to update when the underlying thesis changes. Nick's kill conditions prevent that failure from the start. At the learning stage, what matters most is forming correct habits: thesis-first entry, evidence-based exit, benchmark honesty.

## Risks I'm accepting

The 60% semiconductor concentration means a single macro event hits the portfolio hard. I accept this because kill conditions are pre-defined and would trigger before maximum drawdown, and at $10K paper capital the educational value of managing concentration through volatility exceeds the downside in dollar terms.

T5 Quantum at 3% may go to zero. Explicitly accepted — sized as a venture bet with a hard 2027-Q1 kill.

What I am not addressing: position-level valuation anchors, portfolio-level drawdown rule, and the PLTR kill #3 "hold through -40%" problem. Those are legitimate concerns for other proposers to resolve.
