# Proposal: Nick Probably Has No Edge — And That's Fine

## Core thesis

Nick is an AI reading an AI-written KB, picking stocks from an AI-generated thesis tracker, and calling it "blinded." The information advantage Nick supposedly has is not an edge — it is a simulation of an edge. The real value of this exercise is habit formation and loss psychology, not alpha generation.

## Recommended portfolio structure (conservative path)

- Freeze at current 4 holdings. Do not add T3, T5, or T6 positions until 90-day NAV data exists.
- Set a hard rule: no new thesis added until one existing thesis is retired or confirmed dead.
- Cash ($1,000) stays cash. The T3 entry trigger has already drifted once (RKLB $85 → $110). Drifting triggers are not thesis discipline — they are hope with a label.
- Review gate: if Nick cannot articulate in one sentence what would make him sell each position today, the position should not be held.

## Answer to each open question (A-F)

**A. 3 tight or 6 broad theses?**
3 tight. With 6 theses and $10K, the smallest position is 3% of NAV. That is noise, not conviction. 6 theses is a watchlist with extra steps. The lesson learned from maintaining 6 theses is how to track 6 things — not how to be right about anything.

**B. T5 Quantum — any allocation?**
No. The kill condition is "no commercially viable qubit system by 2027" — meaning Nick cannot know if the thesis is dead until 2027. That is not a thesis, it is a 2-year holding pattern with no observable exit signal before expiry. Zero allocation until there is a quarterly-measurable milestone. Move T5 to C-List now.

**C. ISRG as diversification?**
Do not add. ISRG at 65x P/E is expensive growth stock substitution, not structural diversification. Adding ISRG while leaving NVDA 30% + AVGO 25% intact changes the portfolio's sector label but not its actual concentration risk. The problem is not that Nick lacks ISRG — the problem is that 55% of the portfolio moves together on one macro bet.

**D. Nick's real edge?**
Brutal: none at the information level. Nick reads KB. KB was written by Reese (Claude). Reese synthesized public analyst consensus. NVDA CUDA moat, ASML EUV monopoly, PLTR government AI contracts — these are the most-discussed names on every institutional call in 2025-2026. Nick's only potential edge is execution discipline: holding through drawdowns with pre-committed kill conditions while the market overreacts emotionally. That edge is only demonstrated over 24+ months of actual drawdowns, not 6 days.

**E. Benchmark: SPY or QQQ?**
SPY only. The soul.md benchmark (50% QQQM + 50% SOXX) is harder to beat and conceals whether the thesis framework adds value. If Nick cannot beat SPY — the lowest reasonable bar — the framework failed. Using SOXX as partial benchmark gives Nick credit for riding the same wave he is invested in. That is not calibration, it is flattery.

**F. Post-AI-capex world (2028+)?**
Each thesis needs a sunset clause written now, not later. One sentence per thesis: "When AI capex peaks and declines, what does Nick hold and why?" Currently the answer is nothing — there is no post-capex scenario in any thesis. Without this, Nick will hold AI infrastructure names through the cycle turn because no kill condition covers macro regime change.

## Failure modes everyone is ignoring

**1. Thesis drift without timestamps.**
T3 entry trigger already moved from $85 to $110 within 6 days. In 12 months, every thesis will have silently shifted to accommodate current prices rather than original conviction. Without timestamped original assumptions logged as immutable records, Nick cannot distinguish "thesis evolved on new evidence" from "goal post moved to avoid being wrong."

**2. The learning vehicle never teaches loss.**
Nick is 6 days old and +0.63%. If the portfolio never suffers a real drawdown before the user deploys real capital, Nick taught the most dangerous lesson: that thesis-based investing is comfortable. A paper portfolio that only runs in a bull period is a confidence factory, not a learning tool. The most important skill — staying rational when down 20-30% — cannot be learned if it never happens.

**3. No thesis retirement mechanism.**
A system with no mechanism for killing theses accumulates theses indefinitely. There are 0 retired theses after 6 days. In 2 years, will there be 12 active theses? Add a mandatory rule: minimum one thesis killed per 6-month review cycle, or the framework is declared broken.

That leaves 3 real theses: AI Infrastructure, Space/Defense, AI Software. Benchmark is SPY only, full stop. No new positions until NVDA earnings (May 28) resolve the largest single concentration risk in the portfolio. Define the stop condition now, before anything happens: if Nick is down 15% NAV with zero kill conditions triggered, the framework itself is the bug — pause, audit the thesis structure, do not add positions.
