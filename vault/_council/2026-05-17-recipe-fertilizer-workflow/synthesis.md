## Where proposers AGREE (rare — treat as signal)

1. **No slash commands at start.** All 4 agree: build commands only after manual workflow is validated. Optimist says week 2, others say 4-10 weeks. The unanimous direction is clear: do not automate before the habit exists.

2. **One variable per iteration.** All 4 explicitly or implicitly require changing only one ingredient/variable between versions. This is genuine scientific discipline, not just preference.

3. **Fertilizer needs a longer hold than food.** Even the Caveman distinguishes the two. The only debate is how long: Optimist 60 days, Pragmatist 56 days, Skeptic implicitly 6+ months, Caveman 6 weeks minimum.

4. **Food recipes first to build habit.** Skeptic, Caveman, and Pragmatist all recommend establishing tracking habit with food before staking it on fertilizer (irreversible domain). Optimist doesn't sequence but doesn't contradict this.

5. **Flat log without version files is dangerous for fertilizer.** Even Caveman's own structure, when critiqued, reveals this gap. All sophisticated proposals use separate version files.

## Caveman gut signal

**UNEASY — and the caveman is right about the most important thing**

The gut check caught what the sophisticated proposals missed: food failure costs one meal, fertilizer failure costs a season. Two domains at once when neither tracking habit exists yet is "two hunting trips in opposite directions."

The sophisticated proposals all treat this as a workflow design problem. The Caveman frames it correctly as a **habit formation problem first, workflow design problem second.** This is the structural insight that should anchor the hybrid.

Caveman's gut DOES NOT contradict the Pragmatist on structure — it contradicts the Optimist on timing. The Optimist launches both domains simultaneously on day 1 with KB infrastructure. The gut says: not yet.

## Expertise warnings (Strategist lens)

Three warnings that no proposer raised:

1. **Start a baseline fertilizer log THIS WEEK, before any system is designed.** Every untracked season is permanently lost data. The v0 baseline (current fertilizer, current cost, current palm condition) takes 30 minutes and costs nothing. Do it regardless of which workflow wins.

2. **Recipe development + content creation should be linked from day 1.** The user already has content creation as a goal. Developing recipes on camera (filming iterations, narrating taste decisions) generates compounding value across personal knowledge + content pipeline simultaneously. Add one field: `content_angle:` to the recipe template.

3. **Validate the fertilizer ROI assumption before investing in the workflow.** Organic inputs are often MORE expensive per effective nutrient than synthetic. Cost reduction requires: locally available zero/low-cost inputs (farm waste, rice husks), labor not counted as cost, or yield quality premium in local market. Confirm this assumption holds before building 12+ months of iteration infrastructure.

## Hybrid options

### Hybrid A — Pragmatist core + Strategist modifications (recommended)

Take the Pragmatist's structure (shared spine, status vocabulary, 8-week fertilizer gate, manual-first) and add 3 strategist modifications:

1. **Baseline log FIRST** — before any formula development, document v0 (current practice, current cost, current palm condition). One file, 30 minutes.
2. **Recipe template gets `content_angle:` field** — one sentence on what makes this filmable. Costs nothing, bridges to content goal.
3. **Fertilizer gate extended to 12 weeks** (not 8) — 8 weeks is too short for meaningful palm signal per published data; 12 weeks gives at minimum one intermediate leaf observation and one post-application observation.

Timeline: baseline this week → food recipes week 1-4 (build habit) → fertilizer v1 application week 5 onward → evaluate slash commands at week 8 if habit is established.

### Hybrid B — Caveman minimum + Optimist KB (later)

Start with Caveman's simplest template (just write what you put in + what happened). After 1 month, if habit is established, migrate to Pragmatist structure and add KB integration. This is the lowest risk path — the worst outcome is 1 month of flat logs that never get migrated, which costs nothing.

### Hybrid C — Skeptic sequencing + Pragmatist structure

Run Skeptic's sequencing (recipes first, fertilizer only after 2 months of recipe habit), but use Pragmatist's template and status vocabulary when fertilizer starts. Avoids the Skeptic's "permanent deferral" failure mode by setting a concrete trigger: "start fertilizer after 8 tracked recipe iterations."

## Open questions (must answer before building)

1. Are the palm trees yours alone to test on, or do you need family/farm manager agreement for test plot designation? (Strategist raised this — highest-stakes non-technical risk)

2. What organic input sources are available locally at low/zero cost? (Validates the cost-reduction hypothesis — if inputs must be purchased, the ROI may be negative)

3. Is recipe development intended as personal knowledge, or is content creation a goal from day 1? (Changes the template design and value calculation significantly)

4. What does fertilizer success look like in numbers? e.g., "cost per tree reduced by 20%+ while bunch weight stays within 10% of baseline." (Without this, any outcome can be rationalized as success or failure)

5. Do you currently have a baseline record of what fertilizer is being applied to the palm trees right now? (If not, start this before any other step — regardless of which workflow you choose)
