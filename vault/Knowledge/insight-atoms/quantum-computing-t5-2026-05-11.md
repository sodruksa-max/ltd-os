## IONQ: first quantum company to cross $100M revenue with $470M RPO — commercial momentum unambiguous

**Claim:** IonQ is the only pure-play quantum company with genuine revenue scale ($130M FY2025, +202% YoY; $64.7M Q1 2026, +755% YoY) and a $470M RPO (+554% YoY) representing multi-year contracted revenue — not one-time bookings — validating the commercial transition thesis.
**Evidence:** FY2026 guidance raised to $260-270M (+100%+). Cambridge 256-qubit system sale. KOSTI 100-qubit system sale. $39M HALO Space Dev Agency contract. QuantumBasel $60M+ expansion. Q1 2026 beat guidance by 30%.
**Implication:** IONQ is the anchor of T5 — the only name where commercial momentum is unambiguous. RPO is the most important metric to track: stagnation or decline kills the thesis faster than any earnings miss. Kill condition: RPO stagnates or declines, OR revenue growth <50% YoY for 2 consecutive quarters.
**Source:** IonQ IR Q1 2026, The Quantum Insider, Data Center Dynamics

## QBTS: quantum annealing is commercially viable today — no fault tolerance required

**Claim:** D-Wave's quantum annealing (QBTS) solves constrained optimization problems (logistics, scheduling, drug discovery) on current hardware without requiring fault tolerance, making it the only T5 name with near-term commercial viability that doesn't depend on a 2029 technology milestone.
**Evidence:** $24.6M FY2025 revenue (+179% YoY). $32.8M YTD 2026 bookings including Fortune 100 QCaaS deal. 471% sequential Q4 bookings growth. $32.8M already approaches full-year 2025 totals.
**Implication:** QBTS de-risks the T5 timeline argument — classical computing improvement doesn't close optimization problem advantage the way it might for gate-model computing. Kill condition: classical GPU-based solvers match D-Wave benchmark problems (quantifiable when benchmarks are published).
**Source:** Data Center Dynamics, Motley Fool April 2026
**Date:** 2026-05-11
**Thesis link:** T5

## IBM's qLDPC breakthrough is 1 year ahead of schedule — sets technical credibility floor for the sector

**Claim:** IBM achieved 480-nanosecond qLDPC decoding (10x speedup), one year ahead of schedule, and published Kookaburra (2026: first module with qLDPC memory + Logical Processing Unit) and Starling (2029: 200 logical qubits, 100M error-corrected operations) roadmap — the most credible fault-tolerance path in the sector.
**Evidence:** IBM Newsroom 2025 processor + algorithm breakthroughs. IBM Quantum Blog: Kookaburra Q3 2026 target. Starling 2029: 200 logical qubits + 100M operations = first claim of fault-tolerant quantum advantage in specific workloads.
**Implication:** IBM's roadmap execution sets the sector-wide credibility floor. If IBM demonstrates quantum advantage in even one real workload by end-2026, sector-wide multiple re-rating is possible — including all pure-play names. Kill condition: Kookaburra significantly delayed, OR Google/Microsoft demonstrates practical quantum advantage first (competitive displacement signal).
**Source:** IBM Newsroom, IBM Quantum Blog

## Quantum valuation is the primary risk — RGTI ~1,025x sales, QBTS ~325x sales price in perfection

**Claim:** T5 valuations are extreme: Rigetti ~1,025x trailing sales ❓, D-Wave ~325x ❓, IonQ pricing near-perfection despite only $130M revenue — making valuation, not technology, the primary risk. Any 1-2 year timeline slip triggers severe multiple compression.
**Evidence:** Motley Fool Dec 2025: "quantum computing bubble will burst" — 5 cited reasons: revenue too small, timelines always pushed out, classical computing keeps improving, hype cycles predictable, institutional rotation. Note: ❓ multiples from late 2025 Motley Fool; current figures may differ significantly.
**Implication:** Size T5 for thesis conviction, not market-cap weight. Maximum position sizing should reflect the binary nature of the 2029 fault-tolerance milestone — a lottery ticket, not a compounder. RGTI and QUBT are lottery-ticket tier only; IONQ and QBTS have commercial evidence.

## RGTI laggard + QUBT unverified: two T5 names require extra caution before any position

**Claim:** Rigetti (RGTI) is the structural laggard of T5 — FY2025 revenue declined 34% YoY to $7.1M while peers grew 100-200% — and QUBT has no verifiable revenue with active short-seller allegations and an unconfirmed "$850M cash" figure, making both unsuitable as thesis anchors.
**Evidence:** RGTI: $7.1M FY2025 (-34% YoY) vs IONQ +202%, QBTS +179% — structural underperformance confirmed. QUBT: "$850M cash" is single-source, not confirmed in SEC filings ❓. Short-seller allegation details not surfaced ❓. Wedbush: Neutral rating at $12.
**Implication:** RGTI and QUBT are lottery-ticket allocation only (1-2% NAV max). Do not treat as thesis-anchor names until RGTI shows 2 consecutive quarters of revenue growth, and QUBT confirms cash balance via SEC filing and resolves short-seller allegation. Both require additional verification before any position.
