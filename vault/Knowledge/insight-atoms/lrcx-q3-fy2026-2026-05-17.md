# Insight Atoms: LRCX — Q3 FY2026 Earnings Update

## Gross Margin Recovery Ahead of Schedule — Bear Case #1 Partially Resolved

**Claim:** LRCX Q3 FY2026 gross margin (non-GAAP) came in at 49.9%, exceeding management-guided 48.5% by +140bps.
**Evidence:** PR Newswire LRCX Q3 FY2026 Earnings Release, April 22, 2026. Quarter ended March 29, 2026. GAAP gross margin 49.8% (10bps below non-GAAP = negligible gap).
**Implication:** China revenue replacement from global multinationals is working faster than management's own guidance implied. The primary bear case from May 2026 research (margin compression from 50.6% → 48.5%) is less severe than feared. Full resolution requires Q4 FY2026 (Aug 2026) verification.
**Kill condition relevance:** Kill condition 3 (China recovery stall: GM ≤48.5% in Q4 FY2026) — current trajectory FAVORABLE; not triggered.

## CSBG $2B/Quarter Milestone — Recurring Revenue Buffer Established

**Claim:** LRCX's Customer Support Business Group (CSBG) achieved its first $2.0B quarterly revenue milestone in Q3 FY2026, implying ~$8B annualized run-rate.
**Evidence:** PR Newswire LRCX Q3 FY2026 Earnings Release, April 22, 2026. CSBG = spare parts, upgrades, services from installed base of 80,000+ deployed tools.
**Implication:** CSBG is an annuity-like revenue stream that buffers WFE cyclicality. Even if new system orders soften in a down-cycle, CSBG ~$8B/year provides margin-rich floor revenue. This milestone makes LRCX more resilient to WFE demand cycles than its equipment-only revenue profile suggests.
**Kill condition relevance:** None directly — this is a structural positive.

## EPS Record +41% YoY — Pricing Power Intact Post-China Mix Shift

**Claim:** LRCX Q3 FY2026 non-GAAP EPS = $1.47 (record), +41% YoY, exceeding the top end of management guidance.
**Evidence:** PR Newswire LRCX Q3 FY2026 Earnings Release, April 22, 2026. Revenue $5.84B (+24% YoY, +9% QoQ). EPS growth (41%) outpacing revenue growth (24%) confirms margin expansion or share buybacks amplifying per-share economics.
**Implication:** Despite China customer mix shift (lower-margin customer base exiting), pricing power and cost discipline maintained earnings growth well above revenue growth. This is not a company losing pricing power — it is a company absorbing an export control headwind and still posting record EPS.
**Kill condition relevance:** Kill condition 4 (GM <46% for 2 consecutive quarters) — DISTANT; 49.9% is 390bps above threshold.
