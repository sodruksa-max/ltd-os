# Insight Atoms: AI Capex Cycle 2026

---

## Hyperscaler capex 2026: no pause, all raised or held

**Claim:** All four major hyperscalers (MSFT, GOOGL, AMZN, META) raised or maintained capex guidance in Q1 2026 earnings — combined 2026 guidance is ~$700B, approximately 3x 2024 levels.
**Evidence:** Q1 2026 actuals: GOOGL $35.7B, MSFT $31.9B, AMZN $43.2B. META raised full-year guidance. GOOGL full-year 2026 guidance $180-190B, MSFT ~$190B, AMZN ~$200B.
**Implication:** T1 thesis kill condition (2 consecutive quarters capex below prior year) is NOT triggered. Capex deceleration is not the near-term risk.
**Source:** Fortune April 2026, Stoxcraft April 2026
**Date:** 2026-05-10
**Thesis link:** T1

## AVGO is the structural beneficiary of custom ASIC shift, not NVDA's competitor

**Claim:** AVGO designs and manufactures custom ASICs (XPUs) for GOOGL, META, and ByteDance — it benefits from the custom chip trend regardless of whether NVDA wins or loses training share.
**Evidence:** AVGO AI semiconductor revenue Q4 FY2025: $6.5B/quarter (+74% YoY). Q1 FY2026 guidance: $8.2B. Total backlog: $73B. Confirmed XPU customers: GOOGL (TPU), META (MTIA), ByteDance. Unnamed 5th customer added via $1B order.
**Implication:** AVGO is the safest pick in the custom silicon theme — wins whether spend goes to GPUs or ASICs, because AVGO supplies both paths.
**Source:** Alphastreet, financialcontent/tokenring (Dec 2025)
**Date:** 2026-05-10
**Thesis link:** T1, T2

## ASML is supply-constrained, not demand-constrained: pricing power intact

**Claim:** ASML's constraint is production capacity (how fast it can make EUV machines), not customer demand — giving it structural pricing power through at least 2027.
**Evidence:** ASML Q4 FY2025 backlog: €38.8B. Performance obligations: €46.5B. Demand drivers: TSMC 2nm ramp, Samsung P5, Intel 18A all require additional EUV scanners through 2027. China DUV decline isolated to lower-margin product; EUV unaffected.
**Implication:** ASML moat (T2 thesis) is intact. Supply-constrained = ASML names the price. Cannot be disrupted in the near term.
**Source:** Futurumgroup, ainvest (March 2026)
**Date:** 2026-05-10
**Thesis link:** T2

## TSMC High-NA EUV delay to 2029 shifts ASML's premium upgrade cycle, not its base business

**Claim:** TSMC postponed adoption of ASML's highest-margin product (High-NA EUV) until at least 2029, pushing out ~2-3 years of ASP uplift for ASML.
**Evidence:** TSMC cited cost and capability reasons; will continue with standard EUV through 2028. This affects the marginal revenue premium from High-NA but not EUV volume shipment growth.
**Implication:** ASML 2026-2028 revenue grows on EUV volume (lower margin mix) rather than High-NA (higher margin). Long-term story intact, near-term upside slightly reduced vs prior bull case.
**Source:** MSN/TSMC sourced report
**Date:** 2026-05-10
**Thesis link:** T2

## Overbuild risk is real: Nadella flagged it, depreciation assumptions amplify it

**Claim:** AI infrastructure overbuild risk was publicly acknowledged by a hyperscaler CEO (Satya Nadella, Feb 2026) — and is amplified by aggressive accounting: AI hardware depreciated over 6 years vs 3-year standard for traditional servers.
**Evidence:** Nadella Feb 2026 interview cited "overbuild" risk. Hyperscaler capex now runs at ratios that historically preceded cloud capex pauses. If AI workload revenue doesn't scale proportionally, 6-year depreciation assumption creates impairment cascade risk.
**Implication:** Not a near-term thesis breaker (capex is still rising) but a watch condition. If cloud revenue growth decelerates below 40% YoY for 2 quarters, the accounting risk becomes the story. Monitor every earnings call.
**Source:** Mizuho Insights March 2025, 247wallst April 2026
**Date:** 2026-05-10
**Thesis link:** T1

## NVDA training dominance intact; inference share erosion is directional but slow

**Claim:** NVDA retains >75% share of AI training workloads but is losing inference share to custom ASICs — this erosion is directional (structural trend) not episodic (one-time event).
**Evidence:** AMZN Trainium cited at "$50B annualized revenue run rate potential" (❓ forward estimate, not realized). Analyst projection: custom ASICs 25% of accelerator market by 2035 (❓ Seaport Research projection). T2 kill condition threshold (>15% of training spend captured by alternatives) not yet triggered.
**Implication:** NVDA's long-term TAM compression is real but the timeline is 5-10 years, not 1-2. The near-term thesis is intact. Watch inference revenue disclosures in NVDA earnings for the leading indicator.
**Source:** Sherwood News, Uncoveralpha (April 2026)
