# Insight Atoms: Semiconductor Moats T2 Extended

---

## ARM royalty step-up: Armv8→CSS = 2.5% → 10%+ per chip

**Claim:** ARM's royalty rate steps up from ~2.5% (Armv8) to "north of 10%" (Compute Subsystems/CSS) — every chip designer that upgrades architecture multiplies ARM's per-chip revenue without any increase in ARM's cost base.
**Evidence:** Royalty revenue Q4 2025: $580M (+23% YoY). Full-year revenue exceeded $4B. Gross margins historically above 95% on IP licensing. Kleidi AI layer has 8B+ cumulative installs, creating developer ecosystem lock-in.
**Implication:** ARM's revenue per chip shipped is compounding structurally — more CSS adoption = higher royalty rate = revenue growth even on flat unit volume. The bear case requires RISC-V to win at the design stage before chips ship.
**Source:** BeyondSPX ARM analysis, Motley Fool Nov 2025
**Date:** 2026-05-11
**Thesis link:** T2

## RISC-V is ARM's 5-10 year structural threat, not a near-term disruption

**Claim:** RISC-V's royalty-free model and modular custom AI instruction extensions represent a genuine architectural alternative to ARM, but the disruption timeline for datacenter/AI chips is 5-10 years, not 1-2.
**Evidence:** RISC-V ~25% global unit share Jan 2026 (❓ primarily embedded/IoT, not datacenter). Growing ~80% annually. ARM's own merchant silicon move risks pushing hyperscaler custom ASIC designers toward RISC-V defensively.
**Implication:** ARM T2 thesis is intact for the near term. Watch for Apple/Qualcomm/NVDA announcing RISC-V R&D commitment as the leading kill signal — that is when the 5-10 year risk accelerates.
**Source:** BeyondSPX ARM concentration risk, FinancialContent April 2026

## CRDO owns 73% of AEC market — the fastest-growing AI interconnect category

**Claim:** Credo Technology controls ~73% of the Active Electrical Cable (AEC) market — copper-based rack-to-rack GPU interconnect inside AI clusters — as of Q2 2025.
**Evidence:** Full-stack vertical integration (SerDes IP + Retimer ICs + AEC manufacturing) gives CRDO the lowest-cost, fastest-innovation position. Priced at 17.5x forward P/S vs Marvell at 7.44x — dominance premium is already in the stock.
**Implication:** Near-term hold conviction is high. But CRDO has no photonics roadmap — Marvell's Celestial AI acquisition (close Q1 FY2027) is the signal that optics will eventually challenge copper AEC at short reach. Watch Marvell customer win announcements.
**Source:** Yahoo Finance CRDO vs MRVL, FinancialContent Credo deep dive April 2026

## LRCX has irreplaceable 80%+ share in advanced node etch — content per wafer compounds

**Claim:** Lam Research controls ~80%+ of advanced node etch (high-aspect-ratio, 60:1+) with no competitive alternative at current 3D NAND and GAA node requirements, and its revenue per wafer grows with each technology transition regardless of wafer start volume.
**Evidence:** WFE market growing from ~$110B (2025) to ~$135B (2027) per LRCX CEO. JPMorgan $315 price target citing WFE supercycle. 3D NAND layer count increasing 200→300+ layers means more etch steps even on flat wafer starts.
**Implication:** LRCX is the highest-quality Tier 1 WFE moat in the T2 universe — "content per wafer" growth is the secular bull case inside the memory cycle. Kill condition: 2 consecutive quarters of NAND capex cuts from Samsung/SK Hynix/Micron.
**Source:** Edgen LRCX analysis, 247 Wall St April 2026, Seeking Alpha WFE upgrade
**Date:** 2026-05-11

## AEIS has a cycle-diversifying AI server power business — UCTT does not

**Claim:** Advanced Energy (AEIS) has partially decoupled from the WFE cycle via its Data Center Computing segment (AI server power), which grew 113% YoY to $171.6M in Q3 2025 and now represents 37% of total sales.
**Evidence:** Legacy WFE moat: power delivery systems for AMAT/Lam/KLA with high switching costs (process requalification takes months). New engine: AI server power is WFE-independent. UCTT (OEM sub-supply chain integration moat only) has no equivalent diversifier.
**Implication:** In a WFE downturn, AEIS partially hedges via datacenter power; UCTT has no hedge. AEIS is the preferred sub-tier WFE pick vs UCTT for cycle-aware positioning.
**Source:** BeyondSPX AEIS analysis, EverytTicker UCTT analysis

## ONTO has a technology edge in GAA metrology that KLA currently cannot match

**Claim:** Onto Innovation's spectral ellipsometry with proprietary 3D modeling software has been selected by TSMC, Samsung, and Intel for gate-all-around (GAA) nanosheet metrology — a capability KLA's tools currently lack for this specific architecture.
**Evidence:** KLA has ~55-60% overall inspection/metrology market share but lacks ONTO's advanced 3D modeling for GAA. ONTO's 61% EBIT growth shows the trajectory, but ROIC is currently below WACC — it is a "moat in formation," not a proven durable moat.
**Implication:** ONTO is a speculative thesis play tied entirely to GAA adoption pace (TSMC N2, Intel 18A, Samsung 3GAE). Kill condition: KLA releases competitive GAA metrology tool, or GAA adoption slows. High optionality, not a core hold.
**Source:** GlobalTechResearch process control analysis, SemiAnalysis ONTO vs KLA
